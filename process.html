Delivery model

Phases with clear exits

We break work into reviewable chunks so owners can approve direction before copper, code, and graphics accumulate. Change orders then address real scope shifts—not undocumented drift.

  1. 01
    Discovery & design approval

    Confirm I/O counts, network architecture, SCADA standards, naming, and alarm philosophy. Issue drawings and control narratives for formal or informal sign-off—depending on contract—before major build hours.

  2. 02
    Build & factory acceptance

    Panel assembly, PLC program development, SCADA graphics in the development environment, and witnessed FAT against approved test documents.

  3. 03
    Shipment, installation support & SAT

    Site checkout, loop verification, integration to process, operator training, punch lists, and turnover binders—including backups and license documentation.

  4. 04
    Warranty & sustainment

    Agreed support window for defect correction; optional T&M for enhancements once baseline performance is accepted.

Project management methodology

Formal projects use processes and tools grounded in PMBoK-style practice: project planning, contract administration, execution with monitoring and control, schedule tracking (including MS Project), stakeholder reporting, and closure with documented acceptance—so schedule and commercial status stay visible rather than informal.

Documentation register

We maintain a living list of deliverables: drawing index, software images, test sheets, training attendance, and open punch items. That register becomes the checklist for substantial completion.

Internal automation—same discipline as the plant floor

Project teams use purpose-built tools (custom Excel add-ins, structured databases for parts and requisitions, and CAD-linked workflows) so BOMs, purchase packages, and revision history stay aligned with what ships—mirroring the traceability we build into PLC and SCADA on site.

Change control

Scope changes are quoted or approved in writing before implementation—especially for SCADA graphics and PLC logic that affect interlocks or regulatory reporting.
