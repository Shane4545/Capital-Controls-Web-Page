System architecture

Lead/lag control, level, alarms, and SCADA

Field level signals, panel I/O, PLC sequencing, local operator devices, alarms, and supervisory monitoring.

Typical pump station architecture

  1. Level instrumentationFloats, ultrasonic, or pressure transmitters supply wet well or sump level to the PLC, with discrete backup where specified.
  2. Panel I/O and protectionTerminals, control power, starters or VFD interfaces, and motor protection tie field devices to the controller.
  3. PLC sequencingLead/lag starts, alternation, runtime balancing, and defined responses on fault or high level per the sequence of operation.
  4. Local operator controlDoor HMI, pushbuttons, and selectors show pump status, alarms, and manual or auto mode at the enclosure.
  5. SCADA visibilityRemote monitoring for level, pump run and fault, mode, alarms, and communications health per owner standards.
  6. Fail-safe behaviorAlarms and permissives limit equipment damage and give operators a clear state when inputs or comms fail.

Typical sequence of operation

01

Level rises to demand

Level rises into the start band. The lead pump starts per setpoints and delays defined in logic.

02

Duty / assist response

If level stays high, the lag or assist pump starts on the next band. Status is shown at the door and on SCADA.

03

Alternation and runtime balance

Alternation or runtime balancing rotates duty between pumps per project rules.

04

Alarm and fail-safe behavior

High level, fail-to-start, overload, seal failure, loss of analog signal, and SCADA comms loss raise alarms and drive the specified shutdown or standby response.

05

Operator and remote visibility

Operators use local devices and SCADA for pump status, mode, alarms, and comms health.

Typical platforms

  • Allen-Bradley CompactLogix / ControlLogix
  • FactoryTalk or owner-standard HMI / SCADA
  • Ultrasonic, pressure, and discrete float inputs
  • VFD, starter, telemetry, and packaged motor auxiliaries

HMI / operator control

Local operator interface

Door-mounted HMI, indicators, and controls for local operation when SCADA is unavailable or during field work.

What the operator can see and do

  • See pump run, available, and faulted state without opening the enclosure.
  • Confirm local alarm condition and reset points during field response.
  • Verify manual / auto position during startup and troubleshooting.
  • Support local operation when remote SCADA visibility is limited or unavailable.
