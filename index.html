Signal path

How the control system fits together

A straightforward integration model—field devices, panel wiring, controller, plant network, operator interfaces, and equipment interfaces.

  1. Field instruments & drives Level, flow, pressure, starters/VFDs, and packaged auxiliaries provide the physical inputs and outputs at the process.
  2. Terminations & protection Panel terminals, control power, protection, and segregation route field circuits into controller I/O with labeled points that match drawings.
  3. PLC / PAC Rockwell CompactLogix or ControlLogix (typical) executes sequencing, interlocks, permissives, and alarming per the approved logic narrative.
  4. Plant network Ethernet/IP or owner-standard OT networking ties controllers, HMIs, radios/modems, and supervisory systems where the architecture requires it.
  5. HMI / SCADA Door-mounted HMIs for local visibility; SCADA for alarms, trends, historian points, and remote operations—within owner cybersecurity constraints.
  6. Process & mechanical interfaces Pumps, valves, blowers, chemical systems, and packaged skids receive discrete and analog interfaces defined in the I/O schedule and FAT records.
