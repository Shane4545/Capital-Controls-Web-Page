What we deliver

Engineering services from schematic to SAT

A concise catalog of how we help owners, constructors, and maintenance teams—whether the job is a greenfield MCC lineup or a SCADA faceplate refresh across an existing plant.

What our quotations actually include

The following reflects recurring scope language, BOM structures, and tender packages drawn from how we structure Sales Documents on projects—CCI Quotations, Supplier Quotations, and owner Specifications—not generic marketing language.

For example, on Carleton Place WTP/WWTP (CCI-T-338Q rev D, April 2025), scope of supply is written as: panels, starters (VFD, SS and FVNR) and instrumentation per attached lists—with explicit lines that panel supply includes drawings, submittals, CSA certification and commissioning; PLC/SCADA/HMI panels include software and programming; starters include configuration and commissioning; and instrumentation includes drawings, submittals, configuration and commissioning. Anything not listed as supplied by CCI is outside scope—same discipline we apply to other municipal bids.

  • Div. 25 / instrumentation & controls supply priced distinctly from systems integration labor when the bid calls for it (example: municipal WTP tender packages split “instrumentation & controls” vs integrator total).
  • PLC panels (new or replacement), network / RPU / remote I/O panels, HMI enclosures, and SCADA PC / terminal hardware as specified—coordinated against motor, starter, and MCC schedules.
  • Motor control packages aligned to bid documents: VFD, reduced-voltage / LCS, full-voltage non-reversing, and specialty drives (mixers, blowers, high-service pumps) with tags carried through drawings and PLC.
  • Instrument supply & field commissioning: flow, level, pressure, analytical, and discrete devices—often including manufacturer startup and operator training where quoted.
  • Engineering deliverables bundled in scope: drawings, submittals, PLC/HMI programming, shop testing, and site commissioning through SAT (exceptions—installation labor, valves, confined space—called out explicitly per quote).
  • Coordination with others’ equipment: UV skids, chemical feed, packaged systems—panels and SCADA integrated while mechanical scope remains with the named vendor.
  • Tender hygiene: addenda, spec PDFs, instrument index spreadsheets, combined quotation PDFs, and supplier quote binders maintained under Sales Documents for audit trail.

Representative jobs reviewed for this section include CCI1444 Beachburg WTP Renewal (CCI-T279Q scope of supply), Carleton Place WTP/WWTP (instrument indices, WTP/WWTP panel & starter schedules, PLC references, SCADA terminals), and Golfview subdivision pumping (pump-station quotations and supplier packages).

Electrical schematics & panel design

AutoCAD-based electrical drawings: single-line diagrams, three-line motor schematics, control schematics, loop diagrams, and panel layouts. We maintain revision discipline so site redlines flow back to the master set.

  • Power distribution, motor control, and control power design
  • Terminal block plans, cable schedules, and inter-panel interconnects
  • Coordination with equipment vendors and short-circuit / protection data when provided

Control panel fabrication

Shop wiring to approved drawings, component installation, point-to-point checkout, and factory acceptance testing in our CSA-certified fabrication and test facility. Panels are built for the environment you specify—indoor MCC lineups, outdoor enclosures, and remote kiosk-style installations—and documented to the same quality system we maintain under ISO 9001:2015 (see certifications).

  • PLC, VFD, and networking hardware integration
  • Heat-load and wireway verification against as-built conditions
  • FAT documentation package for owner sign-off

PLC application development

Rockwell-centric development is our default for municipal and industrial work: ladder, structured text, and add-on instructions as appropriate; UDT/AOI patterns for repeatability; and change-controlled program archives.

  • Sequences, permissives, equipment modules, and interlocking
  • Integration to VFDs, motor starters, analyzers, and packaged skids
  • Simulation and dry-run support prior to energization

SCADA / HMI graphics & operations design

We build operator displays in the platforms you operate—FactoryTalk View, AVEVA Wonderware (InTouch / System Platform), GE iFIX, and others on request. Work includes alarm philosophy, faceplate standards, navigation hierarchy, and historian/trend configuration.

  • P&ID-style graphics, equipment pop-ups, and trend groups
  • Role-based security and audit trails per site policy
  • Alarm shelving, flood suppression, and rationalization support

Instrumentation & loop commissioning

Field verification that instruments, valves, and actuators behave as documented in loop sheets and cause-and-effect matrices. Factory-trained technicians with Red Seal credentials perform calibration, verification, and startup for temperature, flow, pressure, and related loops—using current-generation calibrators so records stand up to audit.

  • Scheduled verification programs or one-time commissioning support
  • Loop checks, stroke tests, and calibration records
  • Support during process performance testing
  • Updates to loop folders and redlines after SAT
  • 24-hour on-site service in Eastern & Northern Ontario and Western Quebec where contracted; VPN-based support for remote sites

Product supply & vendor-neutral design

We supply industrial automation components—historically including lines such as Phoenix Contact—and we remain independent: we do not represent manufacturers exclusively, so the architecture and bill of materials reflect your process, not a quota.

Documentation & training

Turnover binders, O&M supplements, loop books, backup media (or secured file transfer), and operator training sessions structured around real plant tasks—not generic software tutorials.
