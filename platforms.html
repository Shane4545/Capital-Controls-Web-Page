Platforms & vendors

Allen-Bradley PLCs, FactoryTalk, Siemens instrumentation—our default municipal stack

Capital Controls standardizes on the Rockwell Automation ecosystem for PLCs and HMI—CompactLogix and ControlLogix are the backbone of most municipal programs we deliver, with Flygt MultiSmart pump controllers integrated where Xylem/Flygt is on the mechanical package. We work hand in hand with Xylem on lift stations, booster skids, and process pumping from coordination through startup. SCADA layers run on FactoryTalk View SE, GE iFIX, or Wonderware/AVEVA depending on your site standard. We supply and commission Siemens process instrumentation as an authorized integrator for Ontario. Networking runs on Ethernet/IP with Allen-Bradley Stratix switches and fiber optic links where distance or isolation requires it.

Platforms & suppliers

Certifications & registration

Allen-Bradley PLCs (Rockwell Automation)

Rockwell Automation's Allen-Bradley platform is the primary PLC family for most municipal programs we deliver—CompactLogix on 1769 (5370) and 5069 (5380) platforms for pumping stations, lift stations, and small process systems (dual Ethernet, Device Level Ring, scalable program memory), and ControlLogix on 5570 and 5580 processors for large water and wastewater treatment plants with extensive distributed I/O and SCADA integration (e.g. 1756-L7x through L8x families as specifications require). Programming in Studio 5000 Logix Designer with clear ladder logic, descriptive tag names, and process control narratives so your maintenance staff can follow the logic years later. Site photography elsewhere on this site illustrates real field work; swap in fresh shots when you have client-approved images of current-catalog processors—the integration story is the same.

  • Standard I/O: 1769-IQ16 digital input, 1769-OW16 relay output, 1769-IF8 analog input (4-20mA), 1769-OF4CI analog output
  • Communication: Ethernet/IP primary protocol; Modbus TCP for third-party devices; HART overlay on analog instruments
  • Redundancy: ControlLogix redundant processors and I/O where specified for critical municipal infrastructure

HMI & SCADA software

Allen-Bradley PanelView Plus 7 touchscreens (7", 10", 12", 15") running FactoryTalk View ME for local operator panels—graphical process displays, alarm acknowledgement, setpoint adjustment, and manual control modes. FactoryTalk View SE or GE iFIX for centralized SCADA workstations providing plant-wide monitoring, historical trending, and multi-site visibility. Wonderware InTouch / AVEVA System Platform where your site has existing enterprise agreements or galaxy architectures we need to integrate with.

  • Graphics development: Process mimics, P&ID-style navigation, alarm summary pages, trend displays
  • Alarm management: Priority-based annunciation, acknowledgement tracking, alarm history logs
  • Remote access: Cellular modem panels with VPN for off-site monitoring and troubleshooting

Engineering tools & documentation

AutoCAD Electrical for panel layouts, single-line diagrams, wiring schematics, and terminal schedules—coordinated with PLC tag databases so every field device has a matching I/O assignment and descriptor. Studio 5000 tag export to Excel for I/O lists and loop sheets. All documentation delivered in native format (ACD, RSLogix, FactoryTalk View) plus searchable PDF for operators and maintenance staff without software licenses.

Process instrumentation (Siemens authorized)

Siemens Sitrans process instrumentation as primary vendor—magnetic flow meters (Sitrans FM Mag 5000/6000), ultrasonic level transmitters (Sitrans Probe LU), and pressure transmitters (Sitrans P DS III, P300). Capital Controls technicians are trained and certified by Siemens for commissioning and configuration. Alternate vendors (Endress+Hauser, Hach, YSI) where specified or required for compatibility with existing systems.

  • Flow measurement: Magnetic flow meters (DN50-DN600) for process water, wastewater, chemical dosing
  • Level measurement: Ultrasonic transmitters for wet wells, tanks, clarifiers; float switches for high/low alarms
  • Pressure measurement: Gauge, differential, and hydrostatic pressure transmitters with diaphragm seals for sanitary service
  • Analytical: pH, turbidity, chlorine residual, dissolved oxygen—Hach and YSI platforms
  • Signal standard: 4-20mA with HART digital overlay for configuration and diagnostics

VFD integration

Allen-Bradley PowerFlex 525 and 755 series variable frequency drives for pumps, blowers, and mixers—Ethernet/IP communication to PLC for speed control, start/stop commands, and fault monitoring. ABB ACS580/ACS880 and Danfoss VLT where specified by mechanical consultants or required for compatibility. Integrated bypass contactors for VFD failure, motor overload protection, and current/power trending sent to SCADA.

Flygt MultiSmart & Xylem pump integration

On many stations the mechanical scope includes Xylem Flygt MultiSmart integrated pump controllers—local pump protection, sequencing, and efficiency features with network connectivity back to the municipal PLC. Supplier product overview: Flygt MultiSmart Pump Station Management System (Xylem Canada). We coordinate closely with Xylem through design, panel build, and commissioning: Ethernet and Modbus TCP (and serial where legacy installs require it) for run status, faults, permissives, setpoints, and SCADA faceplates—so operators see one coherent station narrative from Flygt intelligence through Allen-Bradley logic to your HMI layer.

  • Applications: Duplex/triplex lift stations, booster packages, and process pumps where MultiSmart ships on the pump package
  • Interfaces: Alarms, failsafes, and hand/off/auto coordination matched to your PLC standards and municipal specs
  • Delivery: Joint FAT and startup with Xylem field service when programs are structured that way

Panel standards & electrical components

CSA certified to UL 508A standards—all control panels fabricated in-house with third-party inspection and labeling. NEMA 4/4X stainless steel (304/316) for outdoor and corrosive environments; NEMA 3R painted carbon steel where budget or specification allows. Enclosures from Hammond, Hoffman (nVent), Rittal, or Saginaw depending on size and lead time. Interior components: Allen-Bradley circuit breakers (140M series), Phoenix Contact terminal blocks (CLIPLINE) and power supplies (QUINT 24VDC), surge protection on all I/O, panel heaters and thermostats for outdoor installations.

Networking & remote access

Ethernet/IP as primary industrial protocol—native to Allen-Bradley PLCs, VFDs, and HMIs. Allen-Bradley Stratix 5200/5800 managed switches for VLAN segmentation, Device Level Ring (DLR) redundancy, and quality-of-service (QoS) for deterministic control traffic. Fiber optic links (single-mode for >2km, multi-mode for building-to-building) where electrical isolation or distance requires it. Cellular modems (Sierra Wireless, Digi) with VPN for secure remote SCADA access, alarm notification, and off-hours troubleshooting—coordinated with municipal IT for firewall rules and static IP or dynamic DNS.

  • Protocols supported: Ethernet/IP (primary), Modbus TCP, Modbus RTU (RS485 serial), HART (analog instrument overlay)
  • Network architecture: Segregated control and enterprise networks; managed switches with port security and VLAN tagging
  • Remote monitoring: Cellular modem panels for lift stations and remote sites; VPN tunnels for secure access
