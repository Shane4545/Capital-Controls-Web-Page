Software & systems

Rockwell, Wonderware, iFIX—and the networks underneath

We align to the platforms already licensed and supported at your site. The list below reflects our most common stack—including legacy packages still operating in eastern Ontario (RSView, Lookout, and others named in our corporate profiles). If you standardize on something else, say so in the first call.

Rockwell Automation

Studio 5000 / RSLogix across the Logix families—ControlLogix, CompactLogix, GuardLogix where safety is in scope, and Micro800-class controllers when the application fits—plus the surrounding FactoryTalk ecosystem (PanelView terminals, Linx communication, Historian, and related services when your site standard requires them). We follow manufacturer guidance on firmware, add-on profiles for drives and intelligent devices, and structured program organization for long-term maintainability.

  • FactoryTalk View SE / ME for supervisory and machine-level HMI
  • PowerFlex and embedded Ethernet devices over EtherNet/IP
  • Stratix switching and VLAN design where we are the controls lead

AVEVA (Wonderware)

InTouch and System Platform implementations for municipal clients who standardized on Wonderware historically or through enterprise agreements. We respect template and galaxy conventions so your integrator-of-record can sustain the system.

  • Historian and trending aligned to reporting requirements
  • Redundant architectures and failover testing when specified

GE Proficy / iFIX

iFIX graphics, drivers, and SCADA layers for plants that run GE visualization. Work includes picture libraries, alarm configuration, and integration to PLC tags over OPC or native drivers as deployed.

Historians, reports & enterprise visibility

We connect process data to plant historians (vendor-specific or ODBC/SQL paths) so operations and compliance reporting have a defensible trail—without exposing the control network inappropriately.

Industrial networks & cybersecurity hygiene

Ethernet/IP, Modbus TCP/RTU, serial fiber links, and wireless where justified. We document data paths, document firewall rules at a functional level, and coordinate with IT for remote access and patch windows—matching the posture expected by insurers and municipal IT.

How peers structure similar practices

Leading water-sector integrators often highlight authorized relationships, UL panel shops, and multi-vendor SCADA depth—see for example AMR PEMCO’s water/wastewater practice and Huffman Engineering’s municipal focus. We position Capital Controls the same way: platform depth plus field execution, not generic IT services.
