Pumping Station Control Systems

Design, fabrication, programming, and commissioning of municipal and industrial pump stations.

Wastewater and stormwater stations are controlled on wet well level—ultrasonic, pressure, or redundant level paths—rather than operator guesswork. Logic interfaces to plant or regional SCADA for status, alarms, and remote visibility, while hardware and software are specified for unconditioned field environments: moisture, temperature swing, and long service life.

System Overview

A typical pumping station delivered under our scope includes measurement at the wet well, deterministic pump control, annunciation for fault and high-level conditions, and a communication path suited to your telemetry or SCADA architecture.

  • Wet well level — ultrasonic (commonly mounted in a stilling well or under a hatch), hydrostatic pressure, or both for check/backup; scaling, filtering, and fail-to-safe behavior defined in PLC.
  • Pump control — lead/lag (or alternate) sequencing, start/stop bands, minimum runtime, and interlocks derived from level, pressure, or flow where instrumented.
  • High level alarms — discrete and/or software alarms for high wet well, pump fail, seal/thermal faults (as wired), with visible/audible indication at the local HMI or panel.
  • Emergency or override modes — hand-off-auto at the motor starter or VFD, permissive logic for maintenance, and defined behavior under instrument loss or single-pump outage.
  • Remote telemetry / SCADA — Ethernet/IP or serial paths to RTU/cellular gateways or plant historians; alarm forwarding and remote setpoint/view where the specification allows.

What We Deliver

  • Control panel design and fabrication — CSA-rated construction, documented FAT, nameplate and drawing package aligned to site electrical codes.
  • PLC programming — CompactLogix, MicroLogix, Micro800 family, or ControlLogix where the station is part of a larger campus standard; ladder logic structured for maintenance review.
  • HMI development — FactoryTalk View (ME local or SE client context per site); faceplates for pumps, levels, alarms, and mode selection.
  • Instrumentation integration — level, pressure, flow, and pump auxiliaries wired through appropriate I/O and grounded per instrument OEM and site grounding practice.
  • Field commissioning and testing — loop checks, pump rotation and proof-of-performance under level simulation or staged wet well conditions, SCADA checkout with IT/operations.
  • Documentation — I/O lists, loop folders as required, electrical schematics, panel BOM, redlines, and PLC/HMI exports for owner turnover.

Control Strategies

Station logic is site-specific, but recurring patterns reflect how operators actually run lift and storm stations—minimize cycling, equalize wear, and fail predictably.

  • Lead/lag/alternate sequencing — start order and level bands defined so both pumps share duty; alternate on hours, starts, or after each fill/drain cycle as specified.
  • Runtime equalization — optional balancing of accumulated run hours between pumps to spread mechanical wear across the motor and impeller assembly.
  • High level override — if level exceeds a threshold, lag may start regardless of alternation; may also drive a “storm” or “excess flow” alarm to SCADA.
  • Failover conditions — on overload, seal fault, or no-flow (if instrumented), drop the faulted pump, annunciate, and bring the standby if level still demands.
  • Manual / auto modes — local permissive for O&M, remote start/stop from SCADA only when interlocks allow, hardwired E-stop and starter hand where the electrical design requires.

Hardware / Platforms

  • Allen-Bradley CompactLogix & ControlLogix — Ethernet/IP I/O, scalable racks for multi-pump stations and networked sites.
  • Micro800 series — compact installations where I/O count and networking match a smaller footprint.
  • FactoryTalk View — operator screens for levels, pumps, faults, and trends at the panel or kiosk.
  • VFD integration — speed control, torque or feedback points, coordination with PLC permissives and bypass logic.
  • Remote I/O & telemetry — distributed I/O over Ethernet/IP; cellular or fiber paths to SCADA per municipal IT standards.
