Company story

Capital Controls & Instrumentation Inc. was incorporated in 2007 when an experienced team regrouped after the closure of a prior controls division. Since then we have supported capital and maintenance programs across Ontario and adjacent regions—helping operators run treatment plants efficiently, report accurately, and recover quickly when processes trip.

Our work is rooted in municipal water and wastewater, and we apply the same habits—documented logic, testable interfaces, disciplined drawings—to landfill leachate, industrial machinery, boiler and HVAC-related controls, hydro-related infrastructure, airports, and federal / provincial facilities when those jobs fit our technical scope.

What we do (core offering)

We are a complete controls integrator: network planning for OT layers, control system and electrical design, in-house CSA-certified panel fabrication, instrumentation supply and commissioning, and PLC / HMI / SCADA programming across Allen-Bradley, FactoryTalk, iFIX, and Wonderware platforms. Our shop fabricates panels to UL 508A standards with documented test procedures and photo registers that satisfy CSA factory inspection requirements and municipal procurement standards.

  • We do not act as a manufacturer’s representative—platforms and devices are selected for the application, not a catalog quota.
  • Strict standards for ladder logic, structured text, CAD, and SCADA graphics so any member of our team can support any site we commission.
  • Red Seal instrumentation technicians for calibration, verification, and startup—including yearly instrumentation verification programs for owners who need recurring compliance evidence.
  • Siemens authorized for commissioning of Siemens process instrumentation in Ontario—magnetic flow meters, ultrasonic level transmitters, and pressure transmitters configured to specification.
  • Xylem / Flygt MultiSmart—close coordination on lift stations and booster packages so networked pump controllers, Allen-Bradley PLCs, and SCADA present a single operational picture.
  • Emergency response and warranty support—on-site in Eastern & Northern Ontario and Western Quebec where contracted, and 24-hour VPN support for remote sites.
  • Technical partnerships (e.g. Sudbury, Gatineau) extend geographic coverage for larger maintenance programs.

Certifications & quality

These items mirror what belongs on a municipal or industrial pre-qualification form—CSA for panels, ISO 9001:2015 for the management system, and documented QA practices for day-to-day traceability. Shop panels ship with CSA photo registers (e.g. Bishop Water CP-100) alongside test sheets—so what ships matches what was witnessed.

  • CSA-certified panel fabrication and test facility. Panel products carry the CSA mark; the shop maintains the documentation and test discipline CSA factory inspection expects.
  • CSA / ESA–aligned custom enclosures and industrial control assemblies for Canadian deployment.
  • ISO 9001:2015 — Registered quality management system. Certificate of registration and current scope are available for RFP packages, owner audits, and vendor qualification—use the contact page and reference “ISO certificate request.”
  • ISO 45001 — Occupational health and safety management. The long-running public site at capitalcontrols.ca presented an ISO 45001 occupational health and safety registration mark. Request the current certificate, registrar, scope, and expiry for pre-qualification—the same way we issue ISO 9001 PDFs—so your file matches what engineering reviews.
  • Quality assurance program — Documented QA practices covering fabrication, checkout, and turnover (quality assurance program on file for client review).

If your template asks for registrar name, certificate number, or expiry, we provide the official PDF—not a web summary—so your compliance file matches what engineering submits. The ISO 9001 mark above is used under licence for our certified quality management system; follow your certification body’s logo-use rules for colour, size, scope wording, and any required pairing with their mark.
