Where we focus

Water, wastewater, and selective industrial work

Treatment plants and pumping infrastructure are the core of our practice. We also take on other industrial automation when the control problem fits our PLC/SCADA and electrical skill set.

Industries we support

In addition to municipal water and wastewater, our public-facing practice includes landfill leachate and gas-related SCADA, boiler and combustion-related control, general industrial plants, hydro generation–related work, airports, and infrastructure for municipal, provincial, federal, and private clients—consistent with how Capital Controls has described its markets for years.

Water treatment plants (WTP)

Raw water intake, chemical dosing, clarification, filtration, residuals, clearwells, and high-service pumping often span multiple PLCs and a central SCADA. We implement sequences, permissives, and operator interfaces aligned to your process narrative—whether you are modernizing legacy platforms or expanding capacity.

Wastewater treatment plants (WWTP)

Influent structures, headworks, biological processes, secondary clarification, disinfection, solids handling, and effluent monitoring all depend on reliable I/O, interlocks, and alarm design. We support upgrades that must coexist with running facilities: phased cutovers, temporary bypass logic, and documentation that operators and maintenance can follow under outage pressure.

Lift stations, pumping stations & remote sites

Many utilities distribute control across lift stations, booster stations, and well fields. That usually means RTU/PLC at the edge, secure connectivity back to a central SCADA, and consistent graphics and alarm philosophy across sites. We integrate radios, fiber, or VPN paths according to your IT/OT standards.

Industrial & specialty applications

Outside of water/wastewater, we have delivered (and continue to bid) projects such as swing bridge automation, conveyor and material-handling integration, and other industrial machinery where discrete and motion control meet supervisory SCADA. These jobs are scheduled around our municipal workload—not the majority of revenue—but they use the same engineering habits: documented logic, testable interfaces, and clean handover.

Internal tooling & documentation discipline

Our project systems (including AutoCAD-style electrical workflows, PLC I/O utilities, and structured BOM/submittal tooling developed under internal programs) reinforce consistent schematics, wiring exports, and revision control—so field work matches what was approved in design reviews.
