# Image review — before publishing

Use this checklist when selecting or replacing photos and screenshots for the public site.

## Do not use for marketing heroes or above-the-fold

- **Active fault / alarm screenshots** as the primary story (e.g. PLC comms lost, pump fail, UV off) unless the page explicitly discusses alarm design and the image is clearly in context.
- **Legacy HMI captures** that are very low resolution, black-background, or unreadable on mobile.
- **Casual field snaps** with sticky notes, personal labels, upside-down tags, harsh glare, or obvious clutter unless cropped or replaced.
- **Misleading pairings** (e.g. a municipal SCADA still on a “transit” card without qualification).

## Prefer

- **Wide, legible process displays** (neutral or normal operating state), good contrast, one clear focal read.
- **Finished or staged panels**: organized wire ducting, visible CSA/quality discipline, even lighting.
- **Field hardware** that shows scale and integration (racks, enclosures) without embarrassing detail.
- **Vector or high-res drawings** for engineering credibility.

## Each placement

1. **Truth:** Does the caption match the facility/project type?
2. **Client/vendor:** Approved for public use where required?
3. **Size:** Compress JPG/WebP for web; keep a print master elsewhere if needed.

## Inventory

Approved web photos live under `assets/photos/`. Rejected or internal-only shots should stay out of git or in a private archive.
